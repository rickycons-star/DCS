Config Management — Vision Statement
Vision
"To establish a single, automated and continuously enforced configuration management capability across all product platforms — eliminating drift, reducing operational risk, and enabling every team to operate with confidence that their environments are compliant, consistent and audit-ready at all times."
🔒 Consistency
Every environment — development, UAT and production — operates from a single, version-controlled baseline. Configuration is defined as code, not managed by hand.
⚡ Automation
Drift is detected continuously and remediated automatically for known patterns. No manual checks, no delayed discovery. Alerts within minutes, not weeks.
📋 Auditability
Every configuration change is tracked, approved and retained. Compliance teams and internal audit can interrogate any change across any environment at any time.
🛡 Risk Reduction
Config drift is a leading cause of production incidents and security vulnerabilities. Eliminating it materially reduces operational risk and improves regulatory posture.
🤝 Shared Ownership
Config management is a shared discipline — D&CS provides the platform and standards, product teams own their configuration. Clear accountability at every layer.
📈 Continuous Improvement
Maturity improves iteratively. We baseline current state, measure drift reduction monthly, and progressively automate remediation across all platforms.

Config Management — Target State Architecture
The target architecture establishes a fully automated, policy-driven configuration management capability spanning all product functions. The five-stage model below describes the end-to-end flow from definition to remediation.

Configuration Repository
All configuration stored as code in a centralised Git repository. Branching strategy enforces environment promotion (dev → UAT → prod). No manual configuration permitted outside of the repository workflow.
Baseline Management
Approved baselines defined per environment and platform type. Baselines are versioned, peer-reviewed and signed off by the platform owner. Any deviation from baseline triggers an automatic alert within 15 minutes.
Drift Detection Engine
Continuous scanning compares live configuration against the approved baseline. Drift is classified by severity (critical, high, medium) and routed to the appropriate owner. Critical drift triggers immediate incident creation.
Automated Remediation
Known drift patterns are remediated automatically via pre-approved runbooks. Complex or ambiguous drift is escalated to the engineering team with full context. All remediation actions are logged with before/after state.
Change Control Integration
All configuration changes flow through the ITSM change management process. Emergency changes are permitted only with two-person authorisation and must be documented within 24 hours. No undocumented changes.
Audit & Compliance Reporting
Full audit trail retained for the regulatory retention period. Monthly compliance dashboard published to leadership and internal audit. Annual external audit of config management controls built into the operating calendar.
How to Use These Templates
A guide for discovery session facilitators and programme team members.
1
Before the session
Share the domain questions with the Product Lead 5 days in advance. Ask them to nominate a data lead and engineering lead to join. Confirm the session is 90 minutes.
2
During the session
Work through each domain in order. Capture current state verbatim — do not interpret or adjust in the session. Mark questions Red / Amber / Green provisionally.
3
After the session (within 5 days)
Write up current and target state for each question. Share the completed domain views with the Product Lead for RAG sign-off before updating the portfolio heatmap.
4
Update the heatmap
Once RAG is agreed per domain, update the heatmap for that team. The heatmap is the source of truth for programme leadership reporting.
5
Delta analysis
After all sessions complete, review each domain across all teams to identify the most common gaps. These become the priority workstreams for Phase 3 delivery.
