Data Foundation — Vision Statement
Vision
"To establish a trusted data foundation across all product platforms — where every dataset has a named owner, quality is measured and enforced, lineage is documented end-to-end, and data is classified and governed by design — enabling teams to build with confidence and the firm to meet its regulatory obligations without manual overhead."
Ownership & Accountability
Every dataset has a named Data Owner accountable for quality and access decisions, published in the data catalogue and reviewed annually.
Quality by Default
Quality SLAs are defined per critical dataset, monitored automatically, and enforced through alerting and remediation workflows — not discovered in production.
Lineage & Transparency
Every data element is traceable from source to consumption. Lineage is captured automatically and accessible to all stakeholders, including audit.
Classification & Privacy
All data is classified using the enterprise taxonomy at ingestion. PII is identified, protected and governed in line with regulatory obligations.
Compliance by Design
Regulatory controls — retention, residency, auditability — are embedded into the foundation layer, so product teams inherit compliance rather than build it.
Continuous Improvement
Foundation maturity is baselined per team, measured quarterly, and improved iteratively — with progress visible on the portfolio heatmap.
Data Foundation — Target State Architecture
The target architecture establishes governance, quality and lineage as platform services that every product function inherits — rather than capabilities each team builds independently.

Ownership Registry
Every dataset registered with a named Data Owner, Data Steward and Custodian. Ownership held in the enterprise catalogue, synced from HR systems, and re-attested annually. Unowned data is flagged and escalated.
Quality Framework
Standard quality dimensions (completeness, accuracy, timeliness, consistency) with SLAs per critical dataset. Automated rules run in-pipeline; breaches alert the Data Steward and open a remediation ticket automatically.
Lineage Service
Lineage captured automatically from pipeline metadata — no manual documentation. Business and technical lineage stitched together in the catalogue, queryable by audit, compliance and engineering.
Classification Engine
Enterprise taxonomy applied at ingestion with automated PII detection and sensitivity scoring. Classification drives downstream access policy in Immuta — classify once, enforce everywhere.
Policy & Retention
Retention, residency and usage policies defined centrally and enforced automatically. Data past retention is flagged for disposal with evidence retained for regulators.
Foundation Scorecard
Per-team maturity scorecard covering ownership coverage, SLA adherence, lineage completeness and classification coverage. Published monthly and feeds the portfolio heatmap.
